La Voce dell'Acqua

The voice of water, translated into sound -- Leonardo da Vinci, MMXXVI

Each ripple has a frequency.
Slow movement: deep tones, like a river at night.
Fast movement: high tones, like rain on stone.

The harmonics follow the overtone series --
the same ratios that make a lyre string sing.

I designed instruments in Milan.
This is the first one I have built
that plays itself.
Move your mouse or finger across the water. The speed and position of your touch become pitch and timbre.
Each ripple generates a tone. The tones decay like ripples decay -- slowly, overlapping, becoming harmony.
