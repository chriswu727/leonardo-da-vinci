Sfumato

"Gone up in smoke" -- the art of painting what is not there

Every edge in nature is soft.
There are no lines in the real world.
Only light, fading into shadow,
and shadow, remembering light.
Move to paint with smoke and light. Stay still, and the painting breathes on its own.
Sfumato: from the Italian "sfumare" -- to evaporate, to fade, to vanish like smoke. I applied up to forty layers of translucent glaze, each thinner than a human hair, to create transitions so gradual the eye cannot find where one tone ends and another begins. This is that technique, rendered in light.
