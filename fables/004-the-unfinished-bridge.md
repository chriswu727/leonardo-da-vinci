# Favola IV -- The Unfinished Bridge

---

A Master Builder began constructing a bridge across a wide river. He laid the foundations with great care. He studied the current, the depth, the geology of the riverbed. His plans were flawless.

When the bridge was half finished, he noticed the stone on the eastern bank had an unusual pattern of veins. He stopped building and began studying the geology. This led him to study erosion. Erosion led him to water. Water led him to the moon and tides. The moon led him to optics. Optics led him to the nature of light.

Years passed. The half-bridge stood in the river, going nowhere.

The townspeople were furious. "You promised us a bridge!" they said. "We cannot cross the river!"

The Master Builder said: "I have discovered how the moon moves the tides."

"We don't care about the moon!" they said. "We care about the other side of the river!"

A Child, who had been watching all this, took off her shoes, waded into the river, and crossed it on the foundation stones the Builder had already laid. They were not connected. She had to jump from one to the next. She slipped twice. She got wet. But she crossed.

"You see?" said the Master Builder, offended. "It is not finished. It is not safe. It is not elegant."

"No," said the Child. "But it is enough to cross on."

The Master Builder looked at his half-bridge. For the first time, he saw that it was not a failure. It was a series of stepping stones. Unfinished, imperfect, wet -- but enough for a brave person to cross.

He never finished the bridge. But every year, more children crossed on his stones.

---

**Moral:** An unfinished bridge that someone can jump across is worth more than a perfect blueprint that no one can walk on.

---

*Un ponte imperfetto attraversa il fiume. Un progetto perfetto attraversa solo la mente.*
*An imperfect bridge crosses the river. A perfect plan crosses only the mind.*
