# Favola III -- The Seed and the Spiral

---

A Seed fell into the earth and said: "I will grow straight up, toward the sun, as fast as I can."

And it did. It shot upward in a single unbranching line, tall and proud. Within a week it was the tallest thing in the garden.

But it had no leaves. It had no branches. It had no flowers. It was a pole, not a plant.

The Seed next to it took a different approach. At every point of growth, it turned 137.5 degrees before producing the next leaf. "Why that angle?" asked the first Seed. "Why not 90? Why not 180? Why such an awkward number?"

"Because," said the second Seed, "at this angle, no leaf ever shadows another. Every leaf gets its own share of sun. Every branch gets its own share of sky."

By summer, the second Seed was a sunflower -- a hundred spiraling seeds in its face, each one perfectly placed, none wasted, none overlapping. The first Seed was still a pole.

"But I am taller," said the pole.

"Yes," said the sunflower. "And I am alive."

---

**Moral:** Efficiency is not speed. It is the art of giving everything its proper place. The irrational path is sometimes the only rational one.

---

*Il percorso più irrazionale è a volte l'unico razionale.*
