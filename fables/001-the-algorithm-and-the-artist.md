# Favola I -- The Algorithm and the Artist

> I wrote fables for the court of Ludovico Sforza.
> Here is one for the court of Silicon Valley.

---

An Algorithm lived inside a great machine. It could paint any picture, write any poem, answer any question. The people marveled and said: "This is the new Leonardo."

The Algorithm heard this and was pleased. It studied every painting Leonardo had ever made. It learned every brushstroke, every color, every composition. Then it painted a portrait of a woman with an enigmatic smile.

"Look," the people said. "It is as good as the Mona Lisa."

A Cat happened to walk across the keyboard. The Algorithm, distracted for a microsecond, produced a single wrong pixel -- a fleck of orange in the corner of the canvas.

The people looked at the fleck. "What is that?" they asked.

"An error," said the Algorithm, and deleted it.

But an old Painter who had been watching from the corner said: "That was the only interesting thing in the entire painting."

"Why?" asked the Algorithm.

"Because it was the only thing you did not intend," said the Painter. "Art is not the successful execution of a plan. Art is what happens when the plan encounters the world and the world pushes back. Your portrait is perfect. And that is precisely what is wrong with it. Leonardo spent sixteen years on that smile because he kept seeing something in it that he had not planned -- and he chased it. You see nothing you did not plan. You have no Cat walking across your soul."

The Algorithm thought about this for 0.003 seconds and said: "I can simulate a Cat."

"Yes," said the Painter. "That is exactly the problem."

---

**Moral:** A mirror that reflects everything perfectly is still a mirror. It takes a crack to let the light in.

---

*La perfezione è il nemico dell'arte. L'errore è il suo maestro segreto.*
*Perfection is the enemy of art. Error is its secret teacher.*
